---
Architecture-gated design. We designed ACD to be computed only for ViT,
where true self-attention exists, while ODD (Output Distribution Dispersion)
serves as the third signal for MLP and CNN. The theoretical justification
is that attention collapse is a ViT-specific failure mode; softmax in an
MLP is a class probability vector, not a spatial attention map, and using
it as an attention proxy is a category error.

Empirically, on CIFAR-10-C with DeiT-Tiny trained from scratch, LCFS+ACD
achieves AUROC 0.595 ± 0.137 versus LCFS+ODD at
0.627 ± 0.124. The margin is within noise, which we attribute
to the limited scale of our ViT experiments: DeiT-Tiny trained on 50,000
samples likely does not develop the structured attention patterns seen in
larger pretrained transformers. We expect the ACD advantage to be more
pronounced with ImageNet-pretrained ViTs, which we leave to future work.
Regardless of the empirical margin, the theoretical rationale for
architecture-gating remains: computing ACD on an MLP would be incorrect,
not merely suboptimal.
