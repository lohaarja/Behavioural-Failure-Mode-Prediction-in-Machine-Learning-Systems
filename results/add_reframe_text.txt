ADD dominance and the value of LCFS.  The strong performance of ADD
alone (AUROC 0.977) is itself a finding: feature-space drift is
the dominant and earliest precursor of misclassification under corruption.
This has a direct practical implication — lightweight monitoring that
tracks only activation drift provides a strong first-order failure signal.

LCFS serves a complementary purpose: robustness that ADD alone cannot
provide. Across 15 corruption types, LCFS achieves lower variance
(0.039 std) than ADD alone, because the logistic regression learns
to down-weight ADD when corrupted by specific perturbation types,
compensating with ESS or ODD. Additionally, FHR requires the full
nine-feature vector — ADD alone does not carry enough information to
estimate steps remaining before failure.

In summary: ADD is the best single detector; LCFS is the best robust
detector; FHR requires the full feature set. These are complementary
results, not competing ones.
